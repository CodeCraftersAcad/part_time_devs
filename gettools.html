VS Code

GitHub

1. Go to download >>> Git

It should look similar to the image below

2. Choose your operating system

3. Install it to your system

4. Go to >>> GitHub.com and create an account

5. If you have any terminals open close them all and reopen then run git --version in the terminal and hit the Enter key on your keyboard

It should look similar to the image below (you may have to restart your system)

SSH Key

1. Open your terminal

2. Type ssh-keygen -t rsa in the terminal and hit the Enter key on your keyboard

3. The terminal will ask you for a passphrase. (Suggestion: Hit Enter on your keyboard without entering a passphrase)

4. The terminal will ask you to repeat the passphrase. (Suggestion: Hit Enter again on your keyboard without entering a passphrase)

It should look similar to the image below.

5a. Mac Users: Type cat ~/.ssh/id_rsa.pub | pbcopy and hit the Enter key on your keyboard.

5b. Windows Users: Type cat ~/.ssh/id_rsa.pub | clip and hit the Enter key on your keyboard.

6. Nothing will happen in your terminal

7. Open an internet browser. Go to GitHub.com

8. At the top right of the navigation bar click on the image with a dropdown arrow.

It should look similar to the image below.

9. Click on the settings link

10. On the following page in the left panel find and click SSH and GPG keys

11. On the following page find and click the New SSH key button.

12. A form will appear the Title can be anything. In the Key input press command+v for Mac, or Ctrl+V for Windows. If nothing appears go repeat the steps starting at 5a or 5b.

13. Click the Add SSH Key button
